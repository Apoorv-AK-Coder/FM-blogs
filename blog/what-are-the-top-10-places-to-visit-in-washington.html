10 Most popular places to visit in Washington

1. Mount Rainier National Park

Mount Rainier National Park, which is centered on the imposing summit of Mount Rainier, the highest mountain in Washington, is located less than an hour's drive outside of Seattle. Though access to certain parts of this amazing park is restricted by snowy conditions, it remains a popular year-round destination. There are fantastic hiking trails and fields of vibrant wildflowers in the park's Paradise area.

2. Olympic National Park

Olympic National Park is home to some of the greatest activities in Washington State. Situated on the Olympic Peninsula, the park boasts an impressive 922,651 acres of rainforest. The park receives an impressive 100-140 inches of rain annually. The lush splendor that comes with the downpour makes this one of the most gorgeous sites in Washington, so it's worth the risk of getting wet. You may do beachcombing, hiking, camping, and sightseeing very nearby.

3. Spokane

Located around 300 miles east of Seattle, the second-largest city in the state is quickly becoming one of the top tourist destinations in Washington State. Reach Spokane under budget by grabbing cheap flights to Washington. Fantastic summer hiking and winter skiing and snowshoeing are two of Mount Spokane's best-known features. Enjoy the stunning Eastern Washington scenery at the Finch Arboretum and go hiking, horseback riding, or climbing in Riverside State Park.

4. Leavenworth

In this wonderful Bavarian-inspired hamlet nestled into the eastern foothills of the North Cascade Mountains, get your Oktoberfest on. Leavenworth's structures in the alpine style can make you forget you're in the Pacific Northwest. Trekking, camping, skating, skiing, and other outdoor pursuits abound in one of Washington's most picturesque locations, in addition to the town's wonderful German food and beer and several distinctive stores.

5. Snoqualmie Falls

One of the most well-known waterfalls in the state is Snoqualmie Falls, which is less than an hour's drive from Seattle. Fortunately, the only drama you're likely to witness is the breathtaking 270-foot falls into the Snoqualmie River, which you may recall from the opening titles of the popular television series Twin Peaks. Enjoy a complete perspective of the falls with just a.5-mile climb separating the upper and lower viewpoint sites.

6. San Juan Islands

Although San Juan consists of hundreds of islands, both locals and visitors from outside the area appreciate visiting the Orcas Islands, San Juan Island, and Lopez Island. Outdoor activity is a popular attraction on Orcas Island. Go kayaking, reserve a whale-watching boat excursion, or walk up Mount Constitution at Moran State Park. Friday Harbor, a charming seaside hamlet that will win your heart, is a busy community on San Juan Island. Visit Krystal Acres Alpaca Farm to see the largest herd of alpacas in the San Juan Islands, or go whale watching at Lime Kiln Point State Park.

7. Columbia River Gorge

The Columbia River Gorge is located in the center of the Columbia River Plateau, south of the Rattlesnake Hills. The Cascade Mountains, which constitute the border between Washington and Oregon, are cut through by the gorge, which offers breathtaking views from above. The splendor of this specific location is further enhanced by the Multnomah Falls, which at one point plunge straight into the gorge.

8. Lake Chelan

Lake Chelan, the Pacific Northwest's center for sightseeing, outdoor activities, and wine tasting, is located at the foot of Washington's North Cascades Mountain Range. One of the main attractions in the area is visiting vineyards, where you can spend a day drinking and taking in the scenery at locations like Mellisoni Vineyards, Karma Vineyards, and Vin du Lac Winery.

9. Seattle

Seattle, one of the most visited cities in Washington, is a stunning city situated on a narrow isthmus between Puget Sound and Lake Washington. Although Starbucks is the most well-known attraction in Seattle, the largest metropolis in the Pacific Northwest has much more to offer. 10. Mount St. Helens National Volcanic Monument

This Volcanic Monument, located in Southwest Washington, commemorates the location of the 1980 volcanic explosion. Easily reachable from Seattle, Mount St. Helens is an ideal destination for a multi-day excursion as well as an overnight stay, offering an array of captivating and aesthetically pleasing features for tourists. Don't miss the Silver Lake Mount St. Helens Visitor Center, where you can learn about the area's history and the 1980 eruption.

With its untamed coastline, unspoiled national parks, energetic metropolis, and welcoming little towns, Washington offers travelers pretty about everything they could want.
