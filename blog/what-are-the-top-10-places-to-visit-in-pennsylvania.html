1. Bushkill Falls

The sole purpose of tourists visiting Bushkill is to capture the stunning views of the Bushkill Falls which is widely known as the Niagara of Pennsylvania. No doubt, Bushkill Falls serves to be one of the best family destinations ideal for boating and fishing. Nearby the fall, you will also find a nice trail of streams. You are promised a stunning view when the stream falls into a 100-foot cliff. Make sure to carry your camera and capture this rare beauty. Bushkill Falls is widely known for its natural beauty.

2. Amusement Park in Hershey

This is the top attraction for kids and families in Pennsylvania. The entire park expands over an area of 110 acres. Also, the amusement park hosts more than 70 rides. You must hop into 14 amazing roller coaster rides within this park. There are plenty of entertaining themes ideal for both adults and kids. Hershey's Broadway has around ten water attractions. Also, chocolate lovers will definitely love Hershey's chocolates.

4. Fallingwater

Fallingwater, an iconic landmark in Mill Run, Pennsylvania, will take you back to history. Frank Lloyd Wright had constructed it during the 1930s. Due to its seamless structure and build, it has been referred to as a prime example of organic architecture with natural surroundings all over. Situated near a picturesque waterfall, it reflects the beauty of man-made structures and nature. You will be stunned by its innovative design, use of materials, and unique build indoors and outdoors. Every year, its beauty and significance attract visitors from around the world.

5. The Liberty Bell and Independence Hall

Situated at the heart of Philadelphia, you must make sure to visit these two historic landmarks that are must-see attractions for people who have a deep interest in American history. If you do not know, the Liberty Bell stands as an iconic symbol of freedom and is situated in the Liberty Bell Center. On the other hand, Independence Hall is the place where the Declaration of Independence and the United States Constitution were adopted for the first time. Therefore, these attractions have a great history in the United States.

6. Ricketts Glen State Park

This wonderful park is situated at the heart of Pennsylvania and is also known as the State Park. With a massive size, this park expands into three different counties. It is definitely the ideal spot for all nature lovers. In this park, you will come across more than 24 major waterfalls in addition to other smaller falls. Planning a hiking tour to the Falls Trail System is the best way to witness them.

7. Gettysburg

At Gettysburg, get ready to witness the history of the most significant battles ever fought, which is termed as the Battle of Gettysburg after three days of endless fighting paved the way for heavy casualties and the retreat of the Confederate Army in July 1863. At present, Gettysburg National Military Park stands to be a historic site. Make your Flight ticket booking as early as possible. Gettysburg Heritage Center will give you a brief insight into the entire Civil War and how Gettysburg played a role in it. Jennie Wade House must be explored, which is the only place where Gettysburg citizens who fought lived during the battle.

8. Gettysburg National Military Park

Having a deep history, Gettysburg National Military Park in Gettysburg, Pennsylvania, stands to be a prominent Civil War battlefield widely known for the Gettysburg battle that took place in 1863. You must make sure to explore the historic sites, monuments, and memorials. Gettysburg National Cemetery is a famous place where Abraham Lincoln delivered his famous Gettysburg Address. You can also be a part of the guided tours, interactive programs, and living history demonstrations. You will have a great educational experience.

9. Longwood Gardens

You must visit Longwood Gardens which spans over a vast area of 1,000 acres. Witness the lush landscapes, plant life and some unique horticultural displays. Here you will find fountains, conservatories, and outdoor spaces. You must make sure to attend seasonal events and concerts, take part in workshops and attend classes on gardening and floral design. It is a perfect getaway destination if you are looking for some peace.

10. Philadelphia Museum of Art

This world-class museum features some of the most impressive collections of art that will take you centuries back and give an insight into the cultures. Here you will find the works of Vincent van Gogh, Pablo Picasso, Rembrandt, and Marcel Duchamp, among others. Also make sure to visit the iconic "Rocky Steps," from where you will get the best views of the city skyline. The Philadelphia Museum of Art is the best destination for culture and history.
