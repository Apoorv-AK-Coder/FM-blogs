Melbourne is a fantastic city to explore in Australia. The city is known as the capital of culture, coffee, and sport. Whether you are planning for a weekend getaway or a long-awaited holiday, flying to Melbourne does not have to break the bank. There are two major airports in this city, and there is a competitive airline market. Melbourne flight deals are always available, but you must know where and how to grab these deals. This guide will provide you with detailed expert tips, timing strategies, and insider knowledge.

The Golden Rule: When to Book and When to Fly

Timing is the golden tip, and when it comes to securing cheap flights, flying to Melbourne during peak season will always cost more, and that's why flexibility is your greatest asset.

The Cheapest Months to Fly:

Post-Summer Slump (February & March): You can see a significant drop in the prices from February and March, and often see a substantial drop in prices.

Winter Window (May & June): This time weather is superb, and the flight prices are as well. This is the best time to explore the indoor attractions of Melbourne.

Spring Shoulder (September & October): Before the year's end, the pleasant spring months can offer excellent value on flights.

The Most Expensive Months to Fly:

Once you know the most expensive time to fly, it is pretty easy for you to navigate the Melbourne flight deals. Summary holidays like December and January are the peak season in the city, and with two major events, the New Year and Christmas, this time is the peak season to travel. Another time is the school holidays. Flight prices are high during the school holidays.

Best Day of the Week to Fly:

As per the historical data, Tuesday and Wednesday are the cheapest days to depart and return. Friday and Sunday are typically the most expensive days due to the weekend and business travel.

Melbourne's Airports: Tullamarine (MEL) vs. Avalon (AVV)

There are two airports in Melbourne, and it is your choice which airport you want to choose, because some people consider affordable price airports.

1. Melbourne Airport, Tullamarine (MEL)

When you love convenience, international flights, and all major airlines, then you need to consider this airport, and with this airport booking, you can find the cheapest Melbourne flight deals. The major airlines for this airport are Qantas, Virgin Australia, Rex, Jetstar, and all international carriers fly into MEL. This airport is located about 23 KM from the city centre.

2. Avalon Airport (AVV)

Another airport in Melbourne is Avalon Airport, and this is the best for budget Melbourne flight deals for budget domestic travel and flying with Jetstar. This airport primarily serves as a significant hub for Jetstar. The airport is located about 56 km from Melbourne's CBD, near Geelong.

Pro Tips for Finding the Best Melbourne Flight Deals

Be flexible about dates

If you are looking for affordable Melbourne flight deals, then you must be flexible with the travel dates. You can also use flight comparison websites like Faresmatch and select the" flexible dates" or "whole month" view. This will help you find the cheapest days to fly.

Set up price alerts

Yes, this is another excellent idea to find affordable Melbourne flight deals, and once you have a route and a general time frame in mind, then set up the price alert. You will receive an email notification when the price of your flight drops so that you can book at the optimal moment.

Book in the sweet spot

Well, for domestic flights, you need to aim to book the tickets at least 3 to 4 months in advance, and for international flights, you need to book at least 6 to 8 months in advance.
