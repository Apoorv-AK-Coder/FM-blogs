United Airlines pet policy

United Airlines has an extremely inclusive and cooperative attitude towards pet owners. The United Airlines pet policy intends to provide them with all comforts within reason and facilitate the journeys of them and their furry companions. The pet-friendly approach of United Airlines also requires adherence to the rules set down in its policy by travellers to ensure maximum comfort and security of all travellers.

Travellers are urged to make sure that the destinations to which they are travelling allow them to take their pets with them. All animals need to be kept in hard or soft-sided carriers under the seats of their owners. In case a passenger is travelling with two pets, they need to reserve two separate seats next to each other to accommodate both pets and tend to their needs.

Travellers will not be allowed to carry pets in the cargo and must be near their pets at all times. When booking tickets, travellers will need to specify what animal they intend to carry with them. Accordingly, the pet and their owner will receive separate registration numbers in addition to a special bag tag for the pet’s container.

Necessary documents

Travellers should carry proof of rabies vaccination for countries where such paperwork is compulsory due to strict rabies virus laws. Travellers also need to carry all documents such as flight tickets which show that they are allowed to carry pets with them on the flight. In the case of service animals or military or state department animals, registration of the animals must be carried out.

FAQs

When was United Airlines founded?

United Airlines was founded on 6th April in the year 1926 as Varney Airlines from Boise City in Idaho and commenced operations on 28th March in the year 1931. This, the airlines has been in existence for nearly a century.

What are the classes of service provided by United Airlines?

The classes of service provided by United Airlines include Premium Plus Class, First Class, Business Polaris Class, Economy Plus Class, Economy Class and Basic Economy classes. Different combinations of these classes are found on various flights with Business Polaris and Premium Plus classes being exclusively international.
