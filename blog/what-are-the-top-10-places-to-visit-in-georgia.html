Top 10 Thrilling Places to Visit in Georgia

Blog       Top 10 Thrilling Places to Visit in Georgia


Georgia, one of the fifty states of the United States of America, was admitted on January 2, 1788. Located in the southeastern region of the country on the Atlantic Ocean, Georgia has a rich history dating back to mound-building cultures before European colonization. Despite being a medium-sized state, it ranks as the eighth most populous and twenty-ninth richest state in terms of income.

The state capital, Atlanta, is a significant travel destination and a global city, but it is not the only attraction in Georgia. The best time to visit Georgia for cheap flights is during May and June, just before the summer rush. However, this period can be rainy.

Best Spots to Visit in Georgia

Here are some must-visit locations across various cities in Georgia:

Atlanta

  • World of Coca-Cola: A popular museum in Atlanta showcasing the history and culture of Coca-Cola with unique displays.
  • The Fox Theatre Historic District: Home to several preserved buildings of commercial and residential significance.

Columbus

  • Columbus Post Office and Courthouse: Celebrated for its early twentieth-century architecture.
  • The First African Baptist Church: The first of its kind, built in the early twentieth century, with historical significance.

Macon

  • Bibb County Courthouse: Known for its Neoclassical architecture, it has served as the courthouse for Bibb County.
  • The Allman Brothers Band Museum: Also known as the Big House, this museum features an extensive collection of Allman Brothers Band memorabilia.

Brunswick

  • The Sydney Lanier Bridge: A beautiful cable-stayed bridge over the Brunswick River, offering stunning views.
  • The Ritz Theatre: Originally an opera hall, this theater has been transformed into a movie palace and is a key structure in the Brunswick Old Town Historic District.

Savannah

  • The Forsyth Park: A popular leisure spot for both travelers and locals, formerly known as the Military Parade Grounds.
  • The Gingerbread House: Known as the Cord Asendorf House, this historic Victorian structure features unique gingerbread architecture.

FAQs

What are the important airports of the state?

The state of Georgia has two important international airports which are the Savannah Hilton Head International Airport, located at a distance of thirteen kilometres to the northwest of the Savannah Historic District and the Hartfield Jackson Atlanta International Airport located at a distance of sixteen kilometres to the south of Atlanta.

What are the states which share boundaries with Georgia?

Multiple states share boundaries with Georgia. They are Tennessee to the northwest, South Carolina to the northeast, North Carolina to the north, Florida to the south, and Alabama to the west.
