Best wildlife tours in the World

Wildlife tourism will be the largest tourism industry in the future, and rural development and government aides are helping shape the livelihood of those dependent directly on Nature's produce. As we expand our tourism to the awesome terrains of isolated wildernesses, we must ensure that our tourism and activities do not disturb Nature's most beautiful lairs.

Below are some of the best wildlife tours in the world that tourists can experience and relish, provided that they are sensitive to the delicacy of the Natural world:

Galapagos Islands

In Ecuador's protection and jurisdiction, Galapagos Islands, a volcanic archipelago, presents the most vibrant and diverse wildlife possible. Observing the archipelago's diversity, Charles Darwin came to theorize about Natural Selection. The islands are sparsely populated, and the primary language is Spanish. There are 18 major islands in this archipelago.

Wildlife is replete with various animals especially adapted for survival on this island, including finches, frigate birds, Blue Foot, booby, and Sea Lions. Galapagos Tortoises especially adapted to the climate there. The best time to visit Galapagos would be in winter. Tourists can snorkel and experience the Pacific Ocean.

Wildlife vacation packages in Galapagos Islands:

The best wildlife vacation in Galapagos would include a tour of the youngest islands in the archipelago - Isabela, and Fernandina. There are 18 major islands in the archipelago, and the best packages would include all of them, but the most important of these islands would be the Baltra islands and the Bartholomew islands.

Searching the web can help tourists find the best deals that appeal to them and are affordable, but here are some of the integral activities that an ideal package should have:

  • A minimum of 14 days for the entire stay
  • Snorkeling in the Pacific Ocean and also in the local deep lakes
  • Going on a Pacific Ocean cruise
  • Climbing an active Volcano
  • A visit to Santa Cruz's Tortuga Bay

Sumatra Jungle

Sumatra is an island in Indonesia, home to the endangered orangutan, the Sumatra orangutan. This primate is found in the island's famous Gunung Leuser National Park. Other animals in the tropical region include- rhinos, tigers, and elephants. Tourists are encouraged to go on the jungle trek that reaches the peak of Sibayak Volcano and also visit Lake Toba, the deepest volcanic lake in the world.

Zambia Liuwa Plains

Wildebeest is a significant attraction for tourists who go on wildlife tours. These wildebeests are seen migrating yearly in Liuwa Plains National Park, located in the western part of Zambia. Zambia is the perfect place for a family-guided wildlife tour. Animals in the National Park include exotic deer, bison, and the famous wildebeest.

Wildlife vacation packages in Zambia Liuwa Plains:

Tourists traveling to the land of wildebeest migration should look for these in their family-guided wildlife vacation:

  • Bird watching
  • Participating in the local dance and cultural activities
  • Looking for lions and cheetahs
  • Canoeing in the local water bodies of the National Park
  • Going on a safari

North Spitsbergen

North Spitsbergen is in Norway. Covered with ice and snow, this is one of the best places to witness the whiteness of fish-eating polar bears. North Spitsbergen has six national parks actively associated with preserving this polar environment. Animals found here, excluding the polar bear mentioned, are- arctic wolves, arctic foxes, and rare white-skinned tigers. With its various summits, the Tundra landscape provides each family member with the best place for recreation.

Wildlife vacation packages in North Spitsbergen:

Tropical and Arctic wildlife are distinct, and their differences are worth noting. Here are some of the best things which any wildlife vacation package in North Spitsbergen should have:

  • At least a week of exploration of Svalbard
  • Hiking and snowshoeing
  • A visit to the 14th of July Glacier, Smeerenburg, Alkefjellet
  • A visit to Poolepynton, where walrus watching and photographing is guaranteed.
  • Riding a snowmobile

  • Grand Teton National Park, Wyoming
