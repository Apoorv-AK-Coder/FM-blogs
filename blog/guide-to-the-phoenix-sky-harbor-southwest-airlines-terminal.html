Overview of the Southwest Terminal at PHX

The airline operates the services at Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport and Terminal 4 is the operation airport of the airline. Terminal 4 of the airport is known as the "Barry M. Goldwater Terminal”. This is the largest and busiest terminal at PHX and offers modern facilities, a wide range of services and convenient access for passengers. The main services of the airline operate out of the D Gates in Terminal 4 and make it a central hub for the travellers flying with the airline.

What to Expect at the Southwest Terminal

Here’s a breakdown of what makes the Southwest Airlines terminal at PHX stand out:

1. Easy Check-In and Security

The check-in counters are very fast, and Southwest Airlines already has multiple check-in counters at the airport, located on the west side of Terminal 4, that can handle a high volume of passengers.

Self-Service Kiosks:

For added convenience, self-service kiosks are available for printing boarding passes and checking luggage.

TSA Security Checkpoints:

Terminal 4 features multiple security checkpoints, with TSA PreCheck lanes available for expedited screening.

2. Dining and Shopping Options

There are many good dining and shopping options at the retail outlets here, and you can enjoy the local food here as well and taste the Phoenix’s culinary scene with restaurants like Barrio Café and Four Peaks Brewery.

3. Gate Amenities

Southwest gates in the D concourse are equipped with:

Ample seating areas with charging stations.

Free Wi-Fi throughout the terminal.

Interactive displays for flight information and airport updates.

4. Southwest Lounge Access

While Southwest does not operate exclusive lounges, Terminal 4 includes access to The Club at PHX, an independent lounge available to travellers for a fee or via select credit card memberships. Before your flight, enjoy complimentary snacks, beverages, and a relaxing atmosphere.

Parking and Transportation Options

1. Parking at Terminal 4

The terminal has dedicated parking facilities, including the Terminal 4 Garage and East Economy Lot, and there is a shuttle service to and from the terminal.

Rates are competitive, and online reservations can save you time and money.

2. Transportation Services

Rental Cars: A dedicated Rental Car Center with a shuttle service makes renting a car straightforward.

Rideshares and Taxis: Uber, Lyft, and taxi services are readily available outside the terminal.

Public Transit: The PHX Sky Train connects Terminal 4 to the Valley Metro Rail for affordable travel to downtown Phoenix and surrounding areas.

Tips for a Smooth Experience

Arrive Early

First of all, you need to arrive early at the airport. It is recommended that you arrive early for Southwest flights, at least 90 minutes before the domestic departure and 2 hours before the international departure.

Use Southwest’s Mobile App

You can also check the flight status, access your boarding pass, and receive real-time updates via the airline's application.

Pack Smart

You must take advantage of Southwest's generous baggage policy, and this policy allows you to include two free checked bags per passenger.

Stay Hydrated and Charged

You must bring a reusable water bottle to refill at hydration stations and ensure your devices are fully charged using the charging outlets available at the gates.

Nearby Attractions for Long Layovers

If you have time to spare, consider exploring Phoenix during a long layover. First is the Desert Botanical Garden, which is only 14 minutes from the airport. This place is the best beauty of the Sonoran Desert. Another good place, which is a nearby attraction for long layovers at Phoenix airport, is Downtown Phoenix: A hub for arts, dining, and sports, it’s easily accessible via the Valley Metro Rail.
