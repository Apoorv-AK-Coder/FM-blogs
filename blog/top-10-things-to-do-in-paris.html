Top 10 Things to Do in Paris for Romance and Beyond

Blog       Top 10 Things to Do in Paris for Romance and Beyond


Paris is considered the "City of Love." Full of romantic spots for couples, and it itches those unforgettable moments in an entirely different context. Wherever your idea goes—honeymoon, anniversary, or even just a little sprinkle of Paris magic into your relationship—this city has countless places to enhance those romantic feelings. From the cafe around the corner to the sort of icon that all tourists flock to, it has it down cold. 1. For sunset at the Montmartre Sacred Heart Basilica

For that fully romantic experience, go to the Sacre-Coeur Basilica, located on top of the Montmartre hill of Paris.

  • This relatively high location not only offers a sweeping view of the city from down below but also makes it particularly breathtaking at sunset. When the sun dips away, the sky fills with brightly colourful pinks and oranges. They'll come to light the famous skyline of Paris.
  • Imagine that the basilica is calm by its sounds and cradled by the almost bohemian atmosphere of the Montmartre neighbourhood making it an ideal place for very romantic couples.
  • Afterwards, to keep the romance alive in one of the most picturesque districts in Paris, explore its small streets full of cafés and art studios.

2. The Picnic by the Seine River

An expedition for a picnic with a view of the Seine River is usually the most romantic way to enjoy Paris.

  • Picture yourself lounging around the Seine while the Eiffel Tower is wafting in the distance, drinking in the scenery surrounded by fine French indulgences such as fresh baguettes, cheese, and pastries, and relaxing.
  • Whether the place you choose is calm near the Pont des Arts or the busier side by the Tuileries Gardens, the atmosphere created by the tranquillity of the space combined with its surrounding landscape is great for being intimate.
  • Further, it renders to watch the boats glide by as the landmarks of the city are aglow in their faraway distance.

3. A Private Seine River Cruise

If you want that romantic feel, go ahead and take a private boat cruise.

  • Indulge yourself and contemplate beautiful floating vistas of the great Parisian landmarks in a soft, munching glow from the setting or evening sun - Eiffel Tower, Notre-Dame Cathedral, and Louvre - while you glide bobbing down along bisecting the city.
  • Some tour operators combine champagne service with private guides, pampering clients with a luxurious touch of their own making.
  • A sad heart gliding full through the heart of Paris, magical as sparkling lighted cities reflected upon the water. Such an experience mixes romance, charm, and lovable memories in one package.

4. Tour the Secret Passageways of Paris

Paris possesses some charming secret passages to reveal a more intimate side of her.

  • These historic covered arcades, such as Le Passage des Panoramas and Galerie Vivienne, are perfect for couples wanting a kind of quieter, more romantic experience. In the elegant boutiques, cosy little cafes, and antique shops along the passageways, one can stroll in wanderlust.
  • The soft light and the charm of their architecture bring away all this into ancient times, nearly entirely removed from the busy commotion of Paris.
  • In fact, quite wonderful ways to discover hidden gems of beauty in the city and enjoy a peaceful afternoon with loved ones are these hidden little places.

5. Romantic dining experience at Le Jules Verne in the Eiffel Tower

Make a reservation at Le Jules Verne if you want to raise the stakes for a romantic dinner.

  • This Michelin-starred restaurant is perched on the second floor of the Eiffel Tower and beautifully overlooks the entire city of the world, serving nothing but the finest that there is in French cuisine.
  • Quite a sight for sore eyes, it will seem like a queen and a king dining while overlooking the stretching lights of Paris.
  • Don't forget to reserve your seat in advance, as this exclusive spot tends to fill up pretty quickly.

6. Let Us Walk Through the Luxembourg Gardens

Jardin du Luxembourg consists of the finest green areas in Paris.

  • For a lovely romantic stroll, these beautiful gardens make a welcome slow meander through twisted paths, fountains, sculptures, and cascading flowerbeds.
  • When spring and summer arrive, brilliant colours flutter throughout the gardens, making their quiet corners ideal places to sit and relax as well.
  • Renting a boat on the pond of Grand Bassin, or simply walking hand-in-hand across the bridges over the pools, seems like quite a lovely way to enjoy the quiet atmosphere of the gardens together.

7. Experience a Vintage Car Ride through Paris

Nothing is better and more nostalgic than a ride around some vintage cars through the beautiful streets of Paris.

  • It has been offered by various private companies that take you around Paris in chic cars like the Citroen 2CV.
  • Or a vintage convertible ride that offers style and class while cruising past Paris' famous monuments, such as the Arc de Triomphe and the Champs-Elysees, with a glass of champagne in hand.
  • From a personal and glamorous angle, this activity allows you to discover Paris from a completely different viewpoint.

8. An Evening at the Opera Garnier

A promenade to experience for a couple that relishes art and culture is one at the Opera Garnier.

  • The opera house built centuries ago, known for its princely architecture and elegiac internal beauty, has been set for an ever-memorable evening on date.
  • Enjoy the ambience of class and style while watching a world-class ballet, opera or classical concert. Even without a performance, grand staircases can be explored, intricate frescoes can be admired, and an elegant cafe offers a drink.
  • Opera Garnier has the art blend, romance and cultural majesty to make it a great place for an unforgettable Paris night out.

9. Visit the Musee de l'Orangerie Together

For art lovers, the Musee de l'Orangerie offers a close, intimate, and quiet environment to enjoy some famous masterpieces.

  • Nestled right next to the Tuileries Garden, it houses some of Monet's most iconic masterpieces, the Water Lilies, along with paintings created by Picasso, Cezanne, and many others.
  • It is the ideal venue for a quiet romantic date after the peaceful galleries and beauty of the artwork.
  • Stroll through the exhibits with a hand bearing your loved one, marvelling at all of the craftsmanship, then spend some time reflecting upon every glimmering peak held in each masterpiece at your leisure.

10. Take a Cooking Class Together

To learn the wonderful country cuisine in all its important aspects, they could visit Paris.

  • Someday, these couples would bake sweet pastries like macarons or croissants and prepare exquisite meals like coq au vin or those delectable chicken mushrooms with wine, all under the guidance of a fine chef.
  • Apart from the all-important kitchen skills one would pick up there, the couple would enjoy making "real" French dishes together.
  • At the culmination of the workshop, they would enjoy the fare they prepared, and create new recipes to take home to keep the romance alive in their kitchens.

Paris has everything a couple could want in romantic experiences: weather, serene boat rides, or the best fine-dining experiences with views to die for. Exploring the undiscovered parts of the city or just enjoying gourmet food, Paris makes romance feel timeless.
