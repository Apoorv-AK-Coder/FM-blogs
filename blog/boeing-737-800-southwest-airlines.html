Overview of the Boeing 737-800 at Southwest Airlines

The Boeing 737-800 is part of the Next Generation Series, and it is a bigger version of the 737-700, and Southwest Airlines flights widely use this. This aircraft can carry more passengers while still being fuel-efficient and environmentally friendly. The aircraft is designed for better performance, increased seating capacity, and improved passenger comfort.

Aircraft Type: Boeing 737-800

Seating Capacity: 175 passengers

Cabin Layout: Single-class with open seating

Range: 2,935 miles (approx.)

Engines: CFM56-7B turbofan engines

Cruising Speed: 530 mph

Passenger Experience on the Boeing 737-800

1. Spacious and Comfortable Cabin

The Boeing 737-800 is quite different and special for you because this cabin offers comfortable leather seats. Southwest Airlines follows an open seating policy, and passengers can still find ample legroom and overhead bin space for a relaxed flight experience.

2. Open Seating and Boarding Process

Yes, at the Boeing 737-800 of Southwest Airlines, there is an open seating and boarding process because, unlike other airlines that assign seats that assign seats, Southwest Airlines uses an open seating policy. Passengers are assigned a boarding group (A, B, or C) and a boarding position (1-60), which determines when they board the plane. Those who board first have more choices for aisle, window, or exit row seats.

3. In-flight entertainment and Wi-Fi

Southwest’s Boeing 737-800 comes with Wi-Fi connectivity, allowing passengers to browse the internet, check emails, or stream Southwest’s entertainment options. Features include:

Free movies and TV shows on personal devices

Wi-Fi access (for a small fee)

Free live TV, music, and flight tracking

4. Extra Legroom Seating Options

When we talk about the legroom space, we can say that extra legroom space and seating options are available for exit row seating. Although Southwest does not offer first-class seating, the open seating policy allows travellers to select their preferred spot.
