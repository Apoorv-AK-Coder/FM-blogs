What are the Top 10 Places to Visit in Indiana?

Blog       What are the Top 10 Places to Visit in Indiana?


Indiana is one of the fifty states which form the United States of America country. The state ranks fairly high in population and fairly low in area compared to other states of the country. Popularly known as the ‘Hoosier State’, the state is located in the Midwestern region of the country.

The state shares borders with Michigan, Ohio, Kentucky and Illinois. The state is bordered by the Ohio River to its south and the Wabash River to its west. The state is located on the banks of multiple rivers including White, Blue, Wabash, Whitewater, St. Joseph and Maumee which form important riverine systems of the state.

The state can be roughly divided into two main geographical formations which are the Central Lowlands and Interior Low Plateaus. The state mostly experiences a humid and interior continental climate due to its distance from the coastline while its southern part experiences a subtropical climate due to its latitudinal position.

Places to Visit in Indiana

Indiana has multiple tourist spots scattered all over its sizeable territory which makes cheap flights to Indiana all the more attractive. Most of these are located in and around the major cities of the state. Some of them may be historical while others focus on the natural beauty of the state yet others are remarkable for their valuable contributions to the fields of arts, culture, business or sports.

A List of the Top 10 Spots in Indiana

A list of spots that you cannot miss whenever you decide to get flight tickets to the state of Indiana is included below:

Indianapolis - Indianapolis is the capital of the state of Indiana and consequently, houses some of the most important administrative buildings of the state.

  • The Children’s Zoology Museum – The Children’s Zoology Museum located in Indianapolis is one of the most popular child-friendly tourist destinations in the state.
  • Monument Circle – The Indiana State Soldiers’ and Sailors’ Monument, locally known as the Monument Circle, is an important historical building of the state.

Fort Wayne – Fort Wayne is a very important city in Indiana as the second most highly populated city of the state and therefore receives many tourists.

  • The Lincoln Bank Tower – The Lincoln Bank Tower is an interesting feat of Art Deco architecture located in Fort Wayne and is one of the tallest buildings in the state.
  • Embassy Theatre – Embassy Theatre is a well-known performing arts theatre of the city which has been in business since the late nineteen twenties and has made a name for itself both as a movie theatre and in its present role.

Bloomington – Bloomington is another important city in the state of Indiana as one of its highly populated cities and the location of the well-known University of Bloomington.

  • The Eskenazi Museum of Art – The Eskenazi Museum of Art is a well-known art museum of the state known for its contributions to both art and history.
  • The Busirk Chumley Theatre – The Busirk Chumley Theatre is another well-known performing arts theatre in Indiana easily identified by its unique red Indiana marquee.

South Bend – South Bend is an important city in Indiana which covers a significant part of its territory and is among the largest cities in the state.

  • Studebaker National Museum – The Studebaker National Museum is a well-known museum in Indiana and displays various automobiles, military vehicles, wagons and carriages.
  • Four Winds Field – The Four Winds Field at Covelski Stadium is a popular location for hosting baseball games and has hosted multiple games since its establishment in 1987.

Evansville – Evansville is an important city of Indiana as one of its top three most populated cities and its strategic location on the banks of the Ohio river.

  • The Willard Library – The Willard Library located in Evansville houses multiple important and rare books, manuscripts and genealogies in a beautifully built Gothic Revival style building.
  • Four Freedoms Monument – The Four Freedoms Monument is a statue constructed to commemorate the 1941 speech by Theodore Roosevelt about the Four Freedoms.
