Personal items details to know

A passenger can carry one carry-on bag free of cost and one small personal item on board as per the United carry-on baggage policy. Carry-on baggage size should not exceed 22X14X9 inches and must fit in the overhead bin space of the aircraft. The small item should be at most 17X10X9 inches.

Customs & Entry Requirements in United Flights

When travelling internationally, you must go through customs. For domestic travel or trips within the EU, customs checks are typically not required. However, you will still need to pass through security checks, where your ID and documents will likely be verified. It's a good idea to have these documents readily accessible throughout your journey.

Entry requirements vary by country, so it’s essential to research the specific requirements for your destination. Some countries may require a visa, proof of insurance, or even bank statements. Your passport must be valid for the entire duration of your stay, with some countries demanding at least six months of validity beyond your arrival date. Requirements can differ based on factors like the length of your stay, the purpose of your travel, and the regulations set by the destination country.

For the most accurate information, we recommend checking the official government websites of your destination.

Connecting Flights

Connecting flights involves taking multiple flights to reach your destination, which can lead to a longer travel time, especially when direct flights are unavailable.

If your connecting flight is booked on the same ticket, your baggage will usually be checked through to your destination. However, due to potential flight delays, busy airports, and tight layovers, it's possible for you to make your flight while your baggage does not. Therefore, many travellers are opting to travel light and ship their luggage ahead of time.

Be aware that many low-cost airlines do not offer connecting flights, meaning you will need to manage your onward connections independently and collect your baggage after each flight.

FAQs

What are the size limits for carry-on bags?

Your carry-on bag must fit in the overhead bin and should not exceed 9 in x 14 in x 22 in (23 cm x 35 cm x 56 cm), including handles and wheels.

What qualifies as a personal item?

Personal items must fit under the seat in front of you, with a maximum size of 9 in x 10 in x 17 in (22 cm x 25 cm x 43 cm). Everyday personal items include purses, backpacks, and laptop bags.

Are there additional items I can bring for free?

Yes! You can bring a few extra items at no charge, including:

A jacket or coat

An umbrella

Reading materials

Food or other items purchased at the airport

Mobility devices (wheelchairs, canes, crutches)

A car seat, child safety harness, or stroller

A diaper bag and breast pump (even if not traveling with a child)

A camera

What happens if my carry-on or personal item is too large?

If your items exceed the size limits, you may be required to check them at the gate or at check-in, which may incur additional fees.

Can I bring food on board?

Yes, you can bring food purchased at the airport, along with any personal items you may have. If you have any other questions about baggage policies, feel free to ask.
