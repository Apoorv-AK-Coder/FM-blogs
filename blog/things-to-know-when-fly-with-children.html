2. Review security procedures for airports.

It is essential that kids be aware of security and airport procedures prior to boarding the plane.Reviewing these procedures prior to time can help reduce the fear of the unknown as well as making all participants aware of expectations expected from them.Discuss the check-in procedure along with the necessity of patience during long lines.Make it clear that security screening is a crucial step in flying. In the same way, Transportation Security Administration (TSA) personnel are on hand to ensure safety for everyone on the ground.

Points to Be Noted:

  • Review with your child about what their 'job' will be during security at the airport.
  • Instruct children to take responsibility for their own personal carry-on items.
  • Define the process of passing through metal detectors removing the shoes and jackets (kids older than 12 years old)) and placing their items on the belt that moves.
  • Inform children that electronic devices and food items must be delivered to security separately.
  • Then, ensure that the children aren't forgetting to dispose of any bottle juice or water that they might have consumed.

3. Let each child have their own luggage

  • To ensure a smooth flight ensure that each child has an individual backpack with a carry-on bag.
  • I would suggest an unbag or the rolling suitcase as backpacks can be stored at child's feet and make them accessible during flights.
  • Backpacks can also enable children to keep their hands free while traveling through the airport.
  • Let children help pick the things, activities and snacks that they bring in their suitcases. It should contain a blanky as well as a stuffed animal or another comfort item.
  • Then, ask your children to consider whether your bags will permit them to stroll through the airport.
  • If they find it excessively heavy, then they should consider their options and take items out when needed. (This will prevent Mom or dad from ending having to carry it.)

4. Do layers, skip laces

Be prepared for radically changing temperatures while flying. To avoid this, dress your children in comfy layers, with no zippers, buttons or anything else that may hinder them from reaching the bathroom on time. The same principles apply to shoes. Do not tie your shoes if you can because it reduces the time and effort required to take shoes off and on during security checkpoints and rest time. In reality, when it comes to planning a travel with children dress in layers and not having to deal with the hassle knots in shoes is always a great option!

At the Airport

5. Start early

  • One of the most effective ways to fly with kids is to start off with a great start by getting to the airport before.
  • Waiting, lines, and crowds times are constantly changing, therefore this is one those situations in which I recommend to be cautious by arriving early and in-time.
  • Verify the status of your flight prior to departing for the airport. Adhere to the airport's recommended timings for arrival.
  • In general, it is suggested arriving at airport two hours before domestic flights and 3 hours prior to international flights.
  • Remember, you're in kid time, so make sure to plan for bathroom trips that are unexpected and other deviations.

6. Be aware of the place you park when you arrive

If you're driving to the airport using your personal vehicle, consider whether you will park at the airport or offsite in the shuttle lot. Another option is to spend the night prior to leaving at the hotel that has a park-n'-fly option.

Compare costs and the ease of use. Personally, we prefer to take advantage of an offsite shuttle that is located in the same location every time we travel. We've grown accustomed to the regular shuttle schedule with a friendly and helpful staff as well as adequate security.

Once you are comfortable with a specific parking location, make it your primary choice every time to make sure you have a comfortable and relaxing travel experience.

Write down the space and level that you have parked in. You will also be familiar with the steps you'll need to complete to arrange for airport pickup when you return home.

7. Keep travel documents stored and easily accessible

  • Another suggestion for flying with kids is to choose an adult (Mom or Dad) to take charge of all family reservation and travel identification.
  • Keep your entire family's travel records easy to access and organised by keeping them together in a passport case.
  • For example, if I will provide the airline agent five passports My husband is accountable to load the baggage onto the scales and ensuring it is properly tagged. This is the routine we follow each time we go to the airport.
  • Tell the kids in advance that this is a chance to be quiet until the check-in procedure.

8. Discuss who's in which seat prior to taking off on the plane

  • Who's seated by whom? Who is the one who gets the window seat?
  • There's nothing quite like taking off on an airplane and watching children begin to debate these topics on the plane aisle.
  • This may seem simple, but it is important to discuss the seating area each person will be in advance so that there are no arguments and to keep everyone calm.
  • It is also important to ensure that everyone takes their seats, and keep the flow of passengers flowing. (The same is true for the return flight.)
  • Beware of the spread of germs
  • Clean up 'your area' and the kids' area as soon as you reach your seats. It is possible to do this by carrying a travel size bag of Clorox disinfectant wipes to keep in your carry-on bag that you have readily available.
  • Make sure that everyone in your household wash the headrests, armrests, tray table, and the seat belt buckle.
  • While we cannot travel in a bubble of sterility I'm willing to take every precaution to prevent contracting illnesses when traveling.

10. Prepare for pressure from the air.

  • Another tip to fly with children is to talk with them about how variations in pressure could cause their ears to experience "like they're plugged or popping".
  • Once you've gotten through security, buy bottles of water that the children sip throughout the ascent and descent of the plane. This is one of the worst moments for ear pain.
  • Chewing gum or sucking on candy or yawning are all methods to stimulate swallowing, which can ease the discomfort or pain caused by pressure on the ear.
  • A second tip to plan If you are aware beforehand that your child is susceptible to experiencing ear pains when flying, give them ibuprofen or acetaminophen 30 minutes prior to takeoffs as well as landings. Similar to motion sickness medication.

11. Use good flying etiquette

  • While it's not your responsibility to be a good host to everyone on the plane, there are certain responsibilities as parents flying with our children.
  • The common courtesy of not letting kids play with the seats in the front of them, pull hair, run in the aisles or show bad manners are extremely crucial.
  • As parents, we're accountable for the supervision of our children in ensuring they're not disrupting another person's space or causing any inconvenience.
  • If you are attentive to your children's needs, not just will children remain happy and relaxed during the flight and on the plane, but other passengers will too.

  • Although they might be at the tip of your horn be sure to keep your cool while flying with children.
