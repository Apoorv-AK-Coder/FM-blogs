Blog       What to do, Where to Stay, and What to Eat in Maryland, Pennsylvania


We often go on a vacation to many destinations around the globe. But have you ever visited Maryland, located in Pennsylvania? It is a state in the mid-Atlantic region of the United States. The state shares its borders with Virginia and the District of Columbia to its south and west; Pennsylvania to its north; and Delaware and the Atlantic ocean to its east.

The largest city in this state is Baltimore, and the capital is Annapolis. Some occasional nicknames for the state of Maryland are Old Line State, the Free State, and the Chesapeake Bay State. The name is named after Henrietta Maria, the French-born Queen of England, Scotland, and Ireland, who was known then in England as Mary.

What to do in Maryland?

Visiting the Ocean City Boardwalk

Ocean City Boardwalk is one of the famous tourist attractions in Maryland, with a large, colorful entrance gate that arches over its old-fashioned wooden pier. This place offers more than three miles of shops, restaurants, games, rides, arcades, bars, and hotels, all packed into a single walkable location. Carnival games will surprise you with their gigantic stuffed animals, and rides will keep you moving through roller coasters and Ferris wheels. Ocean City Boardwalk is a good choice to fulfill your day if you want an excellent time in Maryland.

Visiting National Aquarium

It welcomes more than 1.5 million visitors annually and frequently tops the list of the best aquariums in the world. It’s a larger-than-life complex that houses millions of liters of water. Its architecture includes everything from little tide pools to massive arched tunnels that will allow you to walk underwater and have a good experience.

Visiting Great Falls

Located on the border of Maryland and Virginia, Great Falls is a series of water features along the Potomac River. Waterfalls are gushing over the rocks; whitewater rapids rush down mountain valleys. It’s a stunning place to experience the power of nature firsthand. Whether you’re into hiking, climbing, kayaking, or rafting, you can get your heart pumping with outdoor activities that will test your resolve.

Visiting Calvert Cliffs State Park

It is on an expansive, windswept landscape where the mountains meet the sea. It’s a top-rated destination for nature photography because of its outstanding views. You can partake in all outdoor activities to pass the time, including hiking, fishing, and swimming. You can take a trip to this destination that offers fun and educational opportunities.

What to eat in Maryland?

Blue Steam Crabs

Blue crab is what most people think when anyone mentions Maryland’s food. The beloved Chesapeake Bay is teeming with crustaceans from spring through fall, which is cherished over the meat. For a great experience, eat them steamed and dusted with farmed seafood spice, Old bay. This is often served directly atop newspaper-covered tables.

Pit Beef

Maryland is considered in the south, so it shouldn’t be surprised it has its barbecue style. Unlike most countries, which call for cooking fatty meat for hours, Maryland’s regional barbecue is made from lean top toast beef that’s minimally seasoned and barely cooked on a grill directly above hot charcoals. The raw to medium-rare pink meat is thinly sliced and is often piled atop a roll with onions and a sauce.

Rock Fish

It is the official fish in Maryland. It is caught more than any other species. It is also known as striped bass in other parts of the country; in Maryland, it is called rockfish because they like to hide in nooks and crannies of reefs and ledges. The buttery white flesh is medium to firm and has a mild flavor, i.e. little sweet and salty. You can find it prepared in various ways in many restaurants across the state, from grilled toasted to steam.

Berger Cookies

German immigrant Hendry Berger invented it in 1835. The cake with vanilla cookies is hand-dipped in fudge icing, giving it a thick and chocolaty cap. Today these are available in every store in Maryland, and it is one of the most delicious cookies.

Lake Trout

It is made from whiting caught in the Atlantic Ocean, and lake trout are covered in crushed crackers or cornmeal. It is fried until crunchy and golden and served hot atop a slice of white bread. Hot sauce can be optional. In and around Baltimore, restaurants called Lake trout, Lake trout 2, and Lake trout 3 are all excellent places to try this food.
