Lufthansa, officially known as Deutsche Lufthansa A. G., is a well-known airline which serves as the flag carrier of Germany. The airline was established over a hundred years ago, in the year 1917 as Deutsche Luft–Reederei. It has diligently provided high-quality services to the nation since it commenced operations in 1919.

The airline changed its name to Deutsche Luft Hansa in 1926 and since then continued in that name. The airline has received global acclaim and a reputation as one of the largest European airlines in terms of both revenue and passengers carried. It has simultaneously carved a name for itself in the world at large with its efficient service.

Lufthansa Airlines Flights

Lufthansa Airlines flights are mostly purchased from the Airbus aerospace corporation. The airlines use both wide-body and narrow-body aircraft of the said aircraft provider. In addition to this, Lufthansa also utilizes Boeing aircraft for wide-body flights. Lufthansa Airlines fleet has been composed of seven different airline families with two of them being from Boeing and the rest from Airbus.

Lufthansa flights follow a unique tradition of naming their aircraft after German cities. This was started in September 1960 and the first of these flights was named after Berlin. The tradition continued and till 2010 almost all major aircraft of the company were named after major cities in Germany with a few exceptions.

The exceptions included Airbus A321 – 100 which was named after a quarter of Germany and Airbus A340 – 300 which was named after two Canadian cities which had provided shelter to Lufthansa flights during an emergency. Later, it was decided that most aircraft would be known as Star Alliance hub cities except those which had been named after Lufthansa hub cities.

How to Check in for Lufthansa Flights?

Lufthansa Airlines check-in is an easy, simplified and straightforward process as the airline focuses on making all processes for consumers cater to their needs. The check-in facilities for Lufthansa Airlines are available at all airports which serve the airlines as long as they adhere to the safety protocols and clear the security checks of the airport.

Travellers can also visit the official website of the airlines which allows them to make all transactions such as reservations and cancellations. The website also provides special services such as finding last-minute flights and tracking the flight you have chosen. One of its services includes allowing travellers to check in for the Lufthansa flight thirty minutes before departure.

FAQs

Where is the headquarters of Lufthansa Airlines located?

The headquarters of Lufthansa Airlines is located in the city of Cologne in the German state of North Rhine Westphalia. The primary hub of the airlines is located in the Frankfurt Airport in Frankfurt with a secondary hub at the Munich Airport in Munich. The main operations base of the airline known as Lufthansa Aviation Centre is located at the former while the Flight Operations Centre is located at the latter.

What are the subsidiaries of Lufthansa Airlines flights?

Lufthansa Airlines has multiple subsidiaries including both airlines and non airlines. The first category includes Austrian Airlines, Brussels Airlines, Eurowings Europe, Lufthansa City Airlines, Discover Airlines, Lufthansa Cargo, Lufthansa Regional, Air Dolomiti, Swiss International Air Lines, Edelweiss Air, AeroLogic, SunExpress, ITA Airways and Lufthansa CityLine.
