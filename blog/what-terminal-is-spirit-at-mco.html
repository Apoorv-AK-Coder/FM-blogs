Spirit Airlines is in Terminal B at MCO?

Well, the terminal of Spirit Airline is Terminal B. Whether you are checking in for a flight, dropping off luggage or arriving in Orlando, you will be using the facilities in Terminal B.

Key Spirit Locations at MCO:

Check-in and Ticketing: Terminal B, Level 3

Baggage Claim: Terminal B, Level 2

Departure Gates: Airside 4 (Gates 70-99)

The Journey to Your Spirit Gate

Orlando airport map involves a main terminal building (where terminal B is located) and four separate "airside: where the departure gates are situated. After checking in and clearing security in Terminal B, passengers must take an automated people mover to reach the Terminal B gate.

Security Checkpoint: You must use the security checkpoint located on the west side of the main terminal (for gates 70-129) to access the APM.

Automated People Mover (APM): Board the APM shuttle train for a short ride to Airside 4.

Departure Gates: Spirit Airlines flights depart from the gates in Airside 4, which include gates 70 through 99.

Always check the boarding pass and the flight information display screen throughout the terminal for the most accurate and up to the date gate assignment for your flight.

Amenities and Services in Terminal B and Airside 4

The main area of Terminal B and the Airside concourse offer a wide range of services to make your travel experience more comfortable. There are many good options for dining, shopping and lounges available at this terminal.

All your queries

1. How do I get to the Spirit terminal at MCO?

Passengers can follow the airport signs for "Terminal B" upon arrival at MCO. If you are being dropped off or direct your driver to the Terminal B departures level.

2. What gates does Spirit use at MCO?

Spirit uses gates in Airside 4, which encompasses gates 70-99. You can use the automated people mover from the main terminal to reach these gates.

3. How early should I arrive for a Spirit flight at MCO?

No doubt that MCO is a busy airport, and it is recommended to arrive at least two hours before a domestic flight and three hours before an international flight. It allows enough time for security screening, check-in and travel on the APM to your gate.

4. Are Terminals A and B connected at MCO?

Don't worry! You don't need to walk so much because both terminals A and B are located within the same main building. You can also easily walk between the two sides on all three levels before passing the gate of security gate.
