Blog       Tips for stress-free travel with pets


Pets are an important part of a person’s life as an integral part of their close circle and one of the building blocks that constitute their family, making travel with pets an important part of a trip. Travellers looking to provide new experiences to their companions will find much value in this article, which offers advice on pet-friendly trips.

The Best Tips for travelling with pets

Some ideas that can prove useful to travellers intending to find best practices for pet travel are included in the list given below to help those who might be contemplating the idea of bringing their furry companion but are not sure about how to navigate the process:

  • The first step for travelling is packing so make sure that you are carrying all the essentials that you might need starting with leashes, collars and harnesses to a first aid kit in case of any injury sustained on the trip and essential medications that might be required before setting off to new locations.
  • Make sure to select locations that will stimulate your pet and allow them to explore the area at their own pace without any danger of injury. Keeping an eye on them from a distance allows your pet to learn about their new surroundings and not feel smothered while being safe under your supervision.
  • Each pet has unique needs that are best understood by their owner and their veterinarian so every pet owner needs to make sure to get the advice of their general pet doctor before setting out on a trip and making sure to carry everything the doctor may recommend for the trip such as prescribed medications.
  • Travellers need to look for pet-friendly establishments while looking for places to stay at the location they intend to visit. An easy way to do this is running a quick search for hotels and resorts that follow a pet-friendly policy while making reservations for a stay and avoiding those that do not follow one.
  • Travellers should try to bring items that hold a special place in the hearts of their pets such as their favourite chew toy, the softest pillow or the bed that puts them to sleep in a few minutes to provide comfort and familiarity in a new environment they are not yet used to as they slowly learn to adjust to it.
  • Travellers should make sure to carry multiple waste bags to dispose of the faecal matter of their pets as this is an extremely important part of both conserving nature and keeping their surroundings clean. Travellers should avoid any open defecation by their pets due to the great risk of infection that poses to the pets’ health.
  • Travellers should make sure to make reservations before the actual travel due to the tendency of some pets to get irritated or uncomfortable during long waits, especially in unknown locations. The situation might worsen if it is a crowded area with many others waiting for their turns making pre-booking of accommodation and services an essential aspect of the journey.

How Can Pets Travel with You?

An important part of travel is the means of transportation you choose and in the case of travelling with pets, this is one of the most important aspects of the trip. The best option for travellers is to avail of private vehicles belonging either to themselves or companies which have a pet-friendly policy as this allows them to make stops whenever the need may arise at whichever spot is suitable without causing an inconvenience to others.

When travelling in cars, travellers should take care of their pets by restraining them to the carrier seat with the help of a seat belt or in a special carrier depending upon the size and physical build of your pet. If possible, travellers should try to remain in their seats with their pets to reduce their anxiety and allay their fears. Pets also respond more positively to car rides if they are habituated to them gradually.

Sometimes, however, making journeys in private vehicles is simply implausible and travellers must resort to public transportation for their needs. For pet flights, travellers should make sure that they choose airlines with pet-friendly policies and take a careful look at the rules and guidelines provided by the airlines in case of travelling with a pet such as specific crates and carriers.

Training You Need before travel

Travellers need to ensure extensive training of their pets before they set out on the journeys so that their pets and their fellow travellers remain comfortable and the pet owners too can remain free of stress. Travellers should start by training their pets for long rides on cars and other vehicles by taking them for shorter rides first.

Objective care

Travellers also need to account for the personal preferences of their pets. This means that the tips that might be applicable for dogs might not be an answer to the question of how to travel with a cat by plane. While there are obvious differences between species travellers will also see variations depending on the personal preferences of the animals.

Conclusion

Travellers will need to be patient and flexible while travelling with their pets making space for their companions and making them welcome and comfortable throughout the journey. The first and foremost concern should be the safety and comfort of your pets and all rest will fall in place with adequate effort and goodwill.
