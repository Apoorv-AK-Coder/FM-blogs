The Big Four: Florida's Primary International Hubs

These four airports handle the vast majority of Florida air traffic and serve as major gateways for both international and domestic travelers.

1. Orlando International Airport (MCO)

Location: Central Florida, Orlando

This airport is good to go for the booking when you want to visit Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, and all major theme parks. Orlando is designated as part of the "Orlando Experience." When we talk about the unique features of this airport, we can say that it is a bright, open space with an on-site Hyatt Regency hotel right in the main terminal, and a convenient tram system to the gates makes it a destination in itself. It is among the busiest airports in the United States. This airport is the central hub for Southwest Airlines and a focus city for Spirit Airlines, Frontier, and JetBlue. You can get a wide array of shuttle buses, rental cars, and ride-sharing options. The Brightline high-speed train connects MCO directly to Miami from this airport.

2. Miami International Airport (MIA)

Location: South Florida, Miami

This airport is ideal for international travel to Latin America and the Caribbean, South Beach, Miami Beach, and cruises from the Port of Miami. In order to know the unique pointers of this airport, we can say that MIA is the primary gateway between the United States and Latin America. Miami International Airport is known for its impressive public art program, featuring murals, sculptures, and installations throughout the terminals. There is an automated train as well that efficiently connects the airport to the Miami Intermodal Center for rental cars and public transport. There are many leading domestic and international airlines that serve this airport, including American Airlines.

3. Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport (FLL)

Location: South Florida, Fort Lauderdale

This airport is best for cruises from Port Everglades, budget travel, and as a less-crowded alternative to MIA. What is a unique thing about this airport? This airport has become a massive hub for low-cost airlines such as Southwest and Spirit. That's why this is the cheapest airport to fly into in Florida. The airport is a significant base of operations for Spirit Airlines and JetBlue. Also heavily served by Southwest, Allegiant, and other budget carriers.

4. Tampa International Airport (TPA)

Location: Gulf Coast, Tampa

This airport is best for access to Gulf Coast beaches (Clearwater, St. Pete), Busch Gardens, and the Tampa Bay region. Well, TPA is consistently ranked as one of the best airports in the U.S. for customer satisfaction. This airport is the central hub of Southwest Airlines.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is the best airport to fly into for Walt Disney World?

The major airport, which is the closest and most convenient airport for this destination, is Orlando International Airport (MCO). It's about a 20 to 30-minute drive.

2. Which airport is the cheapest to fly into in Florida?

The most affordable or cheapest airports to fly into in Florida are Fort Lauderdale (FLL) and Orlando (MCO). These are the two most competitive rates based on airports.

3. What is the best airport for a Florida cruise?

For cruises from Port Everglades, fly into Fort Lauderdale (FLL).

For cruises from the Port of Miami, fly into Miami (MIA).

For cruises from Port Canaveral, Orlando (MCO) is the closest major airport.

For cruises from the Port of Tampa, fly into Tampa (TPA).

4. How many international airports are in Florida?

Florida has approximately 13 major international airports, with Miami (MIA) being the largest and busiest for international travel.
