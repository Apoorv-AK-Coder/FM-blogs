AirTrain JFK Overview, Key Details & More!

Blog       AirTrain JFK Overview, Key Details & More!


The AirTran at JFK Airport is an automated people mover system that connects passengers between terminals, parking lots, hotel shuttles, nearby public transportation networks in New York City, and rental cars. Therefore, this is a fantastic facility for travelers who are at JFK Airport and looking for automated movers. AirTrain's service is available 24 hours a day. This is one of the significant and essential facilities at JFK Airport that you can explore.

Key Details:

It operates 24/7, year-round.

Free for travel between terminals and airport parking areas.

Fares:

If you are traveling within JFK Airport, then there is no charge, and parking/terminal lots are free. On the other hand, if you are traveling to/from Jamaica or Howard Beach station, then parking charges are $8.25, which can be paid with a Metro Card.

Benefits:

Fast, frequent service every 3-5 minutes during peak hours.

Convenient for travelers avoiding traffic congestion.

Environmentally friendly and reliable.

JFK AirTrain Connection & Route

There are two city connections to the Air Train JFK airport, which offers two main routes to the airport. Each requires a paid segment. The two routes are given below-

Howard Beach route – connects to the MTA A subway line.

Jamaica route – connects to the MTA E, J, Z subways and the LIRR commuter rail.

It is an automated driverless light rail system at John F, Kennedy International Airport. It is mainly designed to make getting around the vast airport complex much easier and to connect the airport to the broader New York City public transportation network.

Key Features and Benefits:

  1. AirTrain JFK service is accessible 24/7, and this train runs all day, every day of the year. Hence, it is always available no matter when your flight is.
  2. It is a fully automated train with no drivers, which means consistent and reliable services.
  3. In terms of accessibility, all AirTrain JFK stations and trains are wheelchair accessible and ensure ease of travel for everyone.
  4. This train reduces travel time and stress, especially compared to navigating airport traffic in a car or taxi.

AirTrain Routes:

There are three main lines of Air Train routes, and they share much of the same track within the airport. Here is an overview.

Jamaica Station Route (Green Line):

Purpose: Connect all JFK terminals to Jamaica Station in Queens.

Stops (in order, approximate): Terminal 1, Terminal 4, Terminal 5, Terminal 7, Terminal 8, Federal Circle, then Jamaica Station.

Connections at Jamaica Station:

NYC Subway: E, J, and Z subway lines (connecting to Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Queens).

Long Island Rail Road (LIRR): Commuter rail service to Penn Station (Manhattan), Grand Central Madison (Manhattan), and various points on Long Island. This is often the fastest way to get to Midtown Manhattan.

Buses: Various local MTA bus routes.

Howard Beach Route (Red Line):

Purpose: Connect all JFK terminals to Howard Beach Station in Queens.

Stops (in order, approximate): Terminal 1, Terminal 4, Terminal 5, Terminal 7, Terminal 8, Federal Circle, Lefferts Boulevard, then Howard Beach Station.

Connections at Howard Beach Station:

NYC Subway: A subway line (connecting to Lower Manhattan, Brooklyn, and other parts of Queens).

Buses: Local MTA bus routes.

Lefferts Boulevard Station: This stop serves the long-term parking lots and airport employee parking.
