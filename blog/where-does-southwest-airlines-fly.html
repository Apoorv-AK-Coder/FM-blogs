1. Why is SouthWest also known as a low-cost carrier?

Southwest Airlines has made massive changes in air travel by making it affordable and accessible to passengers worldwide. The airline is famous for offering low fares, utilizing cheaper airports, operating a single fleet type, efficient scheduling, and offering flights to point-to-point routes.

2. What is the boarding process at SouthWest?

Southwest follows an open seating policy. In place of a pre-assigned seat at SouthWest, passengers are grouped into A, B, or C groups and numbered within each group. As the passengers board, they choose any available seat.
