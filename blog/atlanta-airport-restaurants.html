Where to Eat at Atlanta Airport: A Simple Guide to ATL Restaurants

Blog       Where to Eat at Atlanta Airport: A Simple Guide to ATL Restaurants


ATL, or Atlanta International Airport, is among the busiest airports in the United States. When you are traveling through this airport and feeling hungry but not sure what to eat, this guide is invaluable for you. You’re in luck! There are many different options available for places to eat at ATL for travelers. They can enjoy quick snacks to full meals because there are major restaurants available to eat fast food, southern comfort food, and something a little fancy. This airport offers something unique to everyone. Here is a simple guide to help you find the Atlanta airport food map.

Best Restaurants by Concourse

Concourse T (Near Domestic Terminal)

There are many good options to eat at the Atlanta Terminal T, including Panda Express, Atlanta Bread Company, Starbucks, and Chick-fil-A. What can you enjoy in the list of food ranges here? Sandwiches, soups, coffee, and fast Chinese food are also available at this terminal. You can enjoy an ultimate coffee range at Starbucks, and this coffee chain restaurant is famous for its breakfast items, snacks, and coffee. Don’t miss the visit to Chick-fil-A if you are a lover of chicken sandwiches.

Concourse A

People who love burgers, fries, and milkshakes can also visit the Shake Shack at Atlanta Airport Terminal A. This is the most famous restaurant at Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport—the range of juicy burgers you can avail of at Grindhouse Killer Burgers. Also, you can enjoy the delicious Chicken at Chicken + Beer.

Concourse B

The prominent and top restaurants at Concourse B are Paschal’s, Varasano’s Pizzeria, and Einstein Bros. Bagels. You can enjoy the tasty thin-crust pizza here.

Concourse C

LongHorn Steakhouse – Sit-down restaurant with steaks and salads.

Popeyes – Spicy Chicken and Biscuits.

TGI Fridays – Classic American menu with burgers and wings.

Concourse D

The Original El Taco – Tacos and Mexican-style dishes.

Wendy’s – Fast food, burgers, and fries.

Freshens – Smoothies, wraps, and salads.

Concourse E (International)

One Flew South – Upscale dining with sushi and southern-inspired meals. A traveler's favorite!

Sweet Georgia’s Juke Joint – Southern cooking with a music-themed vibe.

Jamba – Smoothies and healthy snacks.

Concourse F (International Terminal)

The Varsity – Classic Atlanta drive-in with hot dogs, fries, and chili.

Pei Wei Asian Diner – Asian noodles and rice bowls.

Illy Café – Italian coffee and pastries.

Need a Quick Snack?

If you are hungry right now at the Atlanta International Airport and need a quick snack, then there are also so many snack stands, coffee shops, and convince stores in every concourse.

The significant quick snack stands are Auntie Anne’s (pretzels), Cinnabon (sweet rolls), and Hudson News (grab-and-go snacks).

Tips for Eating at ATL

Before walking far to any courses, check the available restaurants where you can eat at the Atlanta airport.

You can also download the ATL airport app or visit the official website to learn everything about your departure gate, including restaurants.

There are many fancier restaurants available.

Final Words

There are many good options at the airport, from veg to non-veg, and every type of food you can explore here. Do you want to eat a burger, or are you looking for fries or sushi? There are so many amazing things to eat.
