Fun at Seattle WheelChair

The Seattle Great Wheel is the city's tallest Ferris wheel and a famous tourist attraction. The 175-foot-tall Ferris wheel has 42 well-designed gondolas, including some posh VIP gondolas on the interior. The Seattle Great Wheel, which is suitable for all ages and provides a panoramic view of the Seattle skyline, is a must-see attraction in the city. Fine dining restaurants and spectacular wheel-light shows add to the ambiance, drawing in additional visitors.

Visit Golden Garden Park

The Golden Garden Park is in Ballard, a Seattle neighborhood that includes various hiking trails, marshes, and beaches. It is one of the most popular parks in the Northwest, with the most stunning beach. You and your loved ones will have a great time here this summer. The beach has a bathroom, a boat launch, fire pits, a playground, and picnic spots, among other amenities. Visitors can sunbathe on the sandy beach, fish off the pier, boat, and play volleyball while taking in the breathtaking views of the Pudget Sound and the Olympic Mountains.

Wild Waves Theme Park

Spend a day at the oldest amusement park with your family enjoying the summer vacation. Roller coasters, water rides, thrill rides, family rides, and kiddie rides are the five major divisions of the park's rides and attractions. Wild Waves theme park is known for its roller coaster rides and water coasters, which include Timberhawk - Ride of Prey, The Wild Thing, wave pool, Riptide, Timber Axe, Lumberjack Falls, and others. Aside from some fantastic rides, Wild Waves Theme Park hosts annual festivals such as Fright Fest, a Halloween celebration, and Holiday Lights, which entice visitors with even more fun and excitement.

Head over Blake Island

Blake Island Marine State Park is another jewel of Puget Sound. Between Vashon Island and Bainbridge Island, this island near Seattle is wedged. This island is only accessible by ferries and boasts amazing beach scenery, biking/hiking trails, and a beautiful perspective of Mt Rainier. Blake Island has the most magnificent sunsets in the vicinity, aside from the natural hideouts. Forest creatures and chirping birds are likely to cohabit together in this area. Blake Island, about 15 miles from Seattle, is a great place to visit for a quick break.

Watch the Stars under the Stars

Take advantage of the beautiful summer weather in the Pacific Northwest by watching a movie outside with friends. Westlake Park on Fridays, Movies at the Mural (Seattle Center) on Saturdays in August, and Magnuson Park on Thursdays are among the locations.

Go Kayaking at UW Waterfront

The Waterfront Activities Center (WAC) is located on the banks of Union Bay, Lake Washington's main entrance. They are conveniently located on the south side of Husky Stadium, just a short walk from the University of Washington light rail stop.

They rent kayaks and canoes from spring through fall. Their boats can accommodate anyone from a solitary paddler to a group of four. Paddle around the Union Bay Natural Area alone, with a group of friends, or with the family, appreciating the wildlife such as bald eagles, osprey, and many varieties of waterfowl. Paddle to the Washington Arboretum and take advantage of the protected canals and hiking trails, which provide excellent opportunities for wildlife.

Enjoy Shopping and Nightlife at Capitol Hill

Are you looking for any new threads? There's no better spot than Capitol Hill's colorful, urban playground for fashion boutiques, sneaker shops, eco-friendly retailers, and thrift stores. When the night falls, check to Knee High Stocking Co., Sun Liquor, and Unicorn for all-night festivities.

Dining on the Edge

The Edgewater Hotel's award-winning restaurant, Six Seven, has a beachfront patio that makes you feel like you're floating on Elliott Bay. This contemporary boutique hotel and restaurant is noted for its amazing views of the Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains, as well as traditional Pacific Northwest food such as cedar plank salmon, big Dungeness crab, and shrimp cake. The hotel has hosted rock 'n' roll icons such as Led Zeppelin and The Rolling Stones since it first debuted in 1962.
