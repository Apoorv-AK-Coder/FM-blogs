What are the Top 10 Places to Visit in Miami?

Blog       What are the Top 10 Places to Visit in Miami?


Miami is one of the best-known cities in the United States of America as a popular beach resort on the shores of the Atlantic Ocean. As stated before, the city is visited by many travellers from all over the country including as many as four million visitors in one year alone. This has led to the city being ranked as the second most visited of all cities and metropolitan areas in the country leading to a great demand for flight tickets.

When Can You Get Flights at Low Rates?

The best time to get cheap flights to the city of Miami is the late summer and early fall season. This time of the year is considered an off-peak or low season as most travellers avoid this period due to the high frequency of hurricanes which hit the city during this time. This ensures that travellers who brave all odds and visit during these months get the cheapest deals and discounts.

Top 10 spots to visit in Miami

The list given below includes the ten best spots that you cannot afford to miss when you get cheap flights to Miami.

  1. Little Havana – Little Havana is a very popular tourist attraction of the city of Miami as the neighbourhood celebrates the culture of immigrants and exiles from Cuba who make up a significant part of its population and contribute greatly to the vibrancy of the city’s culture.
  2. The Vizcaya Museum and Gardens – The Vizcaya Museum and Gardens, once known as the Villa Vizcaya is another popular destination for travellers to the city because of its magnificent position on Biscayne Bay and lush beautiful gardens on its vast grounds.
  3. The Wynwood Art District – The Wynwood Art District is a popular tourist destination with multiple galleries, museums, collections and the widespread display of street art, all of which aim to preserve and encourage various forms of art through different yet equally important methods.
  4. The Adrienne Arsht Centre for the Performing Arts of Miami–Dade County – The Adrienne Arsht Centre for the Performing Arts of Miami–Dade County, one of the largest of its kind in the country, is a popular venue in Miami City which celebrates and displays various performing arts.
  5. Fort Dallas – Fort Dallas is an important historical structure of the city of Miami which draws many lovers of history each year to visit this military base built out of stone during the course of the Seminole Wars in the earlier part of the nineteenth century.
  6. Bayside Marketplace – No trip to Miami is complete without a little shopping spree at the Bayside Marketplace of the city which is situated on the banks of the picturesque Biscayne Bay and contains two storeys worth of items that are perfect for your shopping cart including beach wear and souvenirs for friends and family.
  7. Kaseya Centre – The Kaseya Centre is an important structure of the city of Miami due to its location on the Biscayne Bay and its function as a multi-purpose arena which is known for hosting hockey and basketball games and even concerts from time to time.
  8. Port of Miami – The Port of Miami, once known as the Dante B. Fascell Port of Miami is an important and picturesque seaport located in Miami city where the Miami River meets the Atlantic Ocean and a major commercial hub for the whole country is one of the largest passenger ports of the world and one of the largest cargo ports of the country.
  9. The Maurice A. Ferré Park – The Maurice A. Ferré Park, once known as the Bicentennial Park and still referred to as the Museum Park is a popular recreation spot of the city with its lush greenery and proximity to other important and popular public locations such as the Bayside Marketplace.
  10. he Miami-Dade Zoological Park and Gardens – The Miami–Dade Zoological Park and Gardens is a popular destination for travellers of all ages but especially children to the city who wish to learn more about various animals and plants present in the building which is one of the biggest zoos of the state.

FAQs

What is the airport of Miami called?

The airport of Miami which receives most flights to the city is known as the Miami International Airport and is located in the city’s metropolitan area. Other important airports serving the city are the Fort Lauderdale International Airport located at a distance of twenty-one miles to the North of Miami and the Palm Beach International Airport located in West Palm Beach.

What was the pre-colonial history of Miami?

The area known today as Miami had been the home of an indigenous tribe known as the Tequesta for many years before they migrated to the Cuba region in the mid-eighteenth century. The tribe had lived in that area for over two thousand years and established a village of several hundred people at the place where the Miami River meets the Ocean.
