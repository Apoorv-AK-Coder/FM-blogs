FAQs

What is a boarding pass?

A boarding pass is an essential document provided by an airline that authorises a passenger to board a specific flight. It contains vital details about the passenger, the flight, and the overall travel itinerary.

What are the two primary types of boarding passes?

The two primary types of boarding passes are:

  • Traditional Paper Boarding Pass: This paper document is issued at the airport counter or kiosk and includes the passenger's name, flight information, seat assignment, and a barcode for scanning.
  • Electronic Boarding Pass: This digital version can be accessed on a mobile device or printed at home and features a QR code or barcode for scanning.

What information is usually included on a boarding pass?

A boarding pass typically includes:

  • Passenger Name: The full name of the traveler as listed on their identification document.
  • Flight Details: Information such as the airline code, flight number, departure and arrival airports, departure time, and gate number.
  • Seat Assignment: The assigned seat number and the class of service (e.g., economy, business, first class).
  • Boarding Time: The time when passengers are allowed to start boarding the aircraft.

How do you check Southwest Air flight status?
