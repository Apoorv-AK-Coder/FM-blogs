The great traveling experience is waiting for you while flying with Emirates Airlines. This world-class airlines offers services for more than 136 destinations right now. The baggage policy rules are also not hard and fast for this airline, and this airline provides a stress-free journey experience with good baggage rules. It is essential to understand the baggage size and weight limits of Emirates Airlines for checked and carry-on luggage.

Carry-On Baggage Allowance

Emirates passengers can carry on luggage while following the weight and baggage size limits. Here is the overview.

Economy Class:

Baggage Size: 55 cm x 38 cm x 20 cm (22 in x 15 in x 8 in)

Weight Limit: 7 kg (15 lbs)

Business and First Class

Baggage Size:

One briefcase: 45 cm x 35 cm x 20 cm (18 in x 14 in x 8 in)

One handbag: 55 cm x 38 cm x 20 cm (22 in x 15 in x 8 in)

Weight Limit: 7 kg (15 lbs) per item, totaling 14 kg (30 lbs).

Passengers may carry duty-free items as the carry-on allowance.

Checked Baggage Allowance

If you don’t know, this airline is known for its generous checked baggage allowance, which depends on the route and travel class.

Weight Limits

Economy Class: 20–35 kg (44–77 lbs), depending on the fare type.

Business Class: 40 kg (88 lbs).

First Class: 50 kg (110 lbs).

Piece Concept (Americas and Africa Routes)

Economy Class: Two pieces, up to 23 kg (50 lbs).

Business and First Class: Two pieces, up to 32 kg (70 lbs).

Baggage Dimensions: Each checked bag's total dimensions (length + width + height) must not exceed 150 cm (59 inches). Bags exceeding this limit may incur oversized baggage fees.

Infant Baggage Allowance

For passengers who are traveling with infants, Emirates provides additional allowances:

One extra checked bag (up to 10 kg or 23 kg, depending on route).

A collapsible stroller or carrycot may also be checked free of charge.

Special Items and Sports Equipment

The airline allows passengers to check sports equipment, special items, and musical instruments within the baggage allowance. However, some items, such as bulky items and bicycles, require pre-approval by the airline.

What About Excess Baggage

On the other hand, if your baggage exceeds the free allowance, then additional charges are applicable, and they are based on the passengers' route.

What are the tips for Hassle-Free Travel?

You must weigh your bags before leaving for the airport. Many passengers don’t weigh and check the dimensions of their bags, and as a result, they need to pay the extra baggage fee. You can use the portable luggage scale to avoid expensive surprises at the airport.

Also, make sure to check the airline's route-specific guidelines and policies. They can affect your route, and you must double-check while booking the tickets.

You can also pre-purchase extra luggage, which helps you save money at the airport. Purchasing an additional baggage allowance online is better than making a last-minute payment at the airport.
