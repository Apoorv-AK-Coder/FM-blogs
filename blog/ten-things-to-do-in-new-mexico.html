Below are some of the best things to do in New Mexico.

Visit the White Sands National Park

The white sand national park is located in southern New Mexico. You can do cycling on the road to get a one-of-a-kind experience. Biking is also allowed on the park roads. You can also hike the Dune Trails. Walking among the dunes should not be missed.

You can see the shadowy, jagged peaks of the San Andreas and Sacramento mountains, with the white sand shimmering around you. The White Sands is famous among families. You and your family can sled the edges of the steep dunes before careening down.

Bandelier National Monument

The Bandelier National Park is a broad, beautiful landscape, and it is best to see the historical relics from the ancestral Pueblo tribes that lived and built the area. The place also provides activities like hiking and camping. Outdoor concerts are held. Tours, classes and nature walks are offered by the Museum.

You can also go on sightseeing in the high ridges and rocky caverns. The masonry dwellings with petroglyphs on the walls will give you a brief history of the Native Americans. Bandelier National Monument has a rich history and culture.

Albuquerque International Balloon Fiesta

Albuquerque is one of the best vacation spots in New Mexico. If you visit this place, never miss out on the Balloon Fiesta. It is a large, lovely gathering for hot balloon enthusiasts. The events include coordinated shows, amateur races, and showmanship competitions.

Performances dedicated to New Mexico's indigenous population are also held. You will not miss this event if you enjoy cheap foods, colorful balloons, a festive atmosphere, and unique travel ideas. The place has many fun things to do for couples.

Taos Ski Valley

The place is best known for its dry, rugged desserts. There are many fun things and places to visit in this destination. The valley provides white slopes for skiing, snowboarding and many other activities. Trails for every skill level are provided.

After your outdoor activities, you can enjoy saunas and restaurants with an open fireplace and wood tables in a cozy little village. You can also try hiking, biking, fishing, rafting, rock climbing, horseback riding, and llama trekking. You can also arrange for a hot air balloon ride over the mountains.

Explore the Eldora Craft Chocolate

The factory of Eldora will take you behind the scenes of chocolate creation. The shop will offer you sweet, smooth, and sugary treats. Eldora is best known for its fun, colorful confectioneries. Everything is available here, from Kabow energy bars to adults-only truffles that have been infused with types of alcohol.

You will also find nibs, drops, crunchers, creamers, and chocolate balls. This is a must-visit place for people who are sweet-toothed. Your taste buds will surely enjoy taking a trip to the yummiest New Mexico attractions. Try to book your flights in advance to get a JetBlue flight deal.

Visit the House of Eternal Return

One of the most exciting places to visit in New Mexico in the House of Eternal Return. The place combines the elements of an escape room, scavenger hunt, haunted house, and modern art gallery. Spending a few hours in the place is an entertaining thing to do. The house is complex. The art and architecture are eye-dazzling.

One of the rooms has inter-dimensional portals with lasers and other space-age features. Another room has an Old West theme where hamsters generate a miniature ranch. If you are a big fan of adventure and suspense, this place is the best for you as you never know what you will get when you go to explore the next corner at the House of Eternal Return.

Explore the New Mexico Museum of Art

The Museum of Art has more than 20,000 paintings and is a great place to dive into the culture of New Mexico. Paintings of famous artists like Georgia O'Keeffe, Ansel Adams, and Gustave Baumann are displayed in the Museum.

You will also find an extensive library filled with books and periodicals. The auditorium of the Museum can fit up to 400 people for special events. The place is best for book lovers as well.

Wildlife West Nature Park

The park houses non-releasable animals that have been given sanctuary at the park, and this place serves as a place of conservation. The habitats have been created in such a way that it looks realistic. Looking at the wolves in the grass or foxes in holes, you will look at top-grade mimicry of their natural environments.

Other activities are also there to enjoy in the park. The outdoor amphitheater is used for musical performances. Fall harvest festival and New Mexico Wind Festival have also been hosted in the wide-open fields. If you are an animal lover, you will have a great time with the animals in this park.

Museum of International Folk Art

The Museum has more than 100,000 toys from around the world. All dolls, from tiny handmade Russian dolls to model plane kits from Germany, are displayed. The Museum boasts such a crazy collection that only a tiny portion of dolls, games, puzzles, and miniatures are displayed.

The place is best if you want to relish your inner child. Walking through the front doors and across the wall-to-wall toys, you will realize that you have never really grown up. The place is more than just a tourist attraction.

Cumbres and Toltec Scenic Railroad

You must hop in and aboard the Cumbres and Toltec Scenic Road if you have always wanted to take a Scenic ride through hills and gentle mountains. The train is cute and cozy, and you will surely like the ambiance. It is the world's oldest locomotive.

VIP coaches are available for people who want to travel in luxury. Discount coaches are also available. It offers a cool and unique travel experience and will give you some incredible photos for your vacation memories.
