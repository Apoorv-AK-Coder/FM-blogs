1. Determine Your Needs

First, you need to determine your needs to choose the right check-in bag. Before considering any bag's features and styles, you need to consider your travel habits.

Trip Duration: Short trips require a small bag, and long trips or multi-station trips require a big bag.

Travel Type: Business travellers often need compartments for suits and electronics, while adventure travellers might need rugged and weatherproof bags.

Frequency of Travel: Frequent flyers should invest in durable, high-quality luggage that can withstand repeated handling.

Packing Style: Are you an over-packer or a minimalist? Your packing habits can influence the size and organisation features you need.

2. Choose the Right Size

The airline has specific size and weight limits for the check-in bag. Hence, you need to ensure your choice meets their requirements. Common sizes for check-in bags include:

Medium (24–26 inches): Ideal for trips lasting 3–5 days.

Large (27–30 inches): Perfect for longer vacations or families sharing a single bag.

Extra-large (31+ inches): This size is great for extended trips, but it may be challenging to handle and is more likely to incur overweight fees.

3. Material Matters: Soft-Sided vs. Hard-Shell

Yes, it is another thing that you need to know, and the material of your check-in bag also matters a lot. It affects durability, flexibility, and weight. Here is how it works. For example, soft-sided bags made from fabrics like nylon or polyester are lightweight, flexible, and can expand to fit extra items. They’re perfect for overpackers and travellers who prefer exterior pockets for easy access.

On the other hand, hard-shell Bags are constructed from materials like polycarbonate or ABS. These offer better protection for fragile items and are water-resistant. They’re ideal for travellers who value durability and sleek aesthetics.

4. Look for Essential Features

There are some important features in the perfect check-in bag to enhance and improve convenience and functionality. Here are the must-haves. From wheels to expandable sections, there are many important features that you need to take a look at for the perfect check-in bag. Wheels must be chosen for the spinner wheels for easy use in all directions or inline wheels (two wheels) for durability on rough terrain. You need to check the expandable sections for extra space for souvenirs or last-minute additions. Also, you need to check the TSA TSA-approved locks, which are built-in locks that allow airport security to inspect your bag without breaking it.

5. Prioritise Durability

You need to make sure to prioritise durability because your check-in bag will endure a lot of wear and tear, enduring its journey through airports, baggage handlers, and conveyor belts. Look for:

Reinforced Corners: These protect the bag from damage during impacts.

Quality Zippers: Ensure the zippers are sturdy and smooth to avoid frustrating jams.

Scratch-Resistant Materials: Particularly important for hard-shell bags.

Summary

Choosing the perfect check-in bag is essential for hassle-free travel and keeping your belongings safe. This guide from Faresmatch helps you determine the best luggage tailored to your needs. Factors like trip duration, travel type, and packing style play a significant role in selecting the right bag. Consider size options to match your trip length, material choices like soft-sided or hard-shell for durability and flexibility, and essential features such as spinner wheels, expandable sections, and TSA-approved locks. Prioritising durability with reinforced corners, quality zippers, and scratch-resistant materials ensures your bag can handle the rigours of travel.

FAQs

Why is choosing the right check-in bag important?

What factors should you consider before buying a check-in bag?

Consider your travel habits, such as trip duration, travel type (business or adventure), frequency of travel, and your packing style (over-packer or minimalist).

What are the standard sizes for check-in bags?

Medium (24–26 inches): For trips lasting 3–5 days.

Key features include

Spinner Wheels: For easy manoeuvrability.

Airlines have specific size and weight restrictions for check-in bags, so it’s essential to choose luggage that complies with these limits to avoid additional fees.
