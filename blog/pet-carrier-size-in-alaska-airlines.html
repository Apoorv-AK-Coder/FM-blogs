Pet Carrier Policy of Alaska Airlines

Alaska Airlines is a pet-friendly airline. When flying with this airline you are allowed to bring your pets on your flight. However, there is a standard procedure that you have to follow when carrying your pets. There are proper policies that guide this pet carrier perk. These policies defer for cabin and baggage compartments. A proper classification of Alaska Airlines Pet Policy between these two cabins can be classified as follows-

  1. Cabin Pet Carrier Policies- You can bring a maximum of two pets in the main cabin and three pets in the first-class cabin. The pet carrier must fit into the channel and it must be leakproof, escape-proof, and properly ventilated. The kennel can be made of wood, metal, or hard plastics. There should be enough room for the animal's movement. You can bring dogs, cats, rabbits, and household birds on board. However, with pets, you cannot sit in emergency exit seats or bulkhead rows. You must be eighteen years old to carry a pet along with you on your flight. You have to pay a fee of $100 each way for carrying your pet onboard.
  2. Baggage Compartment Pet Policies- The pet carrier must not weigh more than 150 lbs. One must travel on the same flight as their pets. One can bring their live dog or live cat who is at least 8 months old and weaned or a live puppy who is at least 8 months and 6 weeks old and weighs more than 20 lbs. One is not allowed to bring brachycephalic cats or dogs which would include Persian, exotic Shorthair, Burmese, and Himalayan cats. The pets must have a health certificate signed by a veterinarian not more than 10 days before travel. If one is returning on the same day the certificate can extend up to thirty days old. The fee for a pet in the baggage compartment is $150 each way.
