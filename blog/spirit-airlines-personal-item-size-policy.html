What Counts as a Personal Item?

First, you need to understand the meaning of a personal item, a small bag or an accessory that fits under the seat in front of you. This airline allows passengers to bring one personal item for free and is a cost-effective option for the light-packers. This item typically includes -

Backpacks

Purses

Laptop bags

Small totes

Briefcases

Size Limit for Personal Items

Spirit Airlines has a strict size policy for personal items. To qualify as a free personal item, your bag must not exceed the following dimensions:

18 x 14 x 8 inches (45 x 35 x 20 cm), including handles and wheels.

Size limit is essential to know and ensures that the item can fit comfortably under the seat in front of you. Any bag more significant than these dimensions will be considered a carry-on or checked bag, and you must pay an additional fee.

What Happens if Your Bag is Too Big in Spirit Airlines?

If your bag is too big at Spirit Airlines, you must pay the extra carry-on or checked bag price at Spirit Airlines. The baggage fee is higher when paid at the gate. Thus, if you have extra baggage, pre-pay for the bags to avoid additional charges.
