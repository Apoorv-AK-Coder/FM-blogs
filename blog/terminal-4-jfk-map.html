What is Terminal 4 at JFK?

When you think of Terminal 4 as a bustling mini-city within JFK Airport, It is a vast, modern building that serves as a significant hub for Delta Airlines and many other international flag carriers. This terminal has been popular at the New York airport because it has many shops, restaurants, and lounges. Why is T4 essential? It is a primary gate for travellers flying to and from destinations all around the world, especially across Europe, Asia, and other continents.

How is Terminal 4 Laid Out (The "Map")?

There are two main areas for the gates of Terminal 4, and here are these –

Concourse A – This concourse features gates A2 through A21, and it handles many international airlines, including Delta connection flights.

Concourse B: It is among the larger concourses with gates B20 through B55, and Delta primarily uses it for both its domestic and international flights as well as by its SkyTeam partners.

When we talk about connectivity, we can say that both concourses are connected to the main terminal building, where you'll find check-in, security, and arrivals.

How Do I Get to and From Terminal 4 at JFK?

You can get to Terminal 4 at JFK using AirTrain JFK, which is an automated monorail. This train connects T4 directly to all other JFK terminals.
