Blog       Top 5 Underrated Travel Destinations


Not all travel destinations all over the world are equally well known among travellers and often slip under the radar of those excited to find new places to visit and appreciate and this article can serve as a great help to all these travellers who are looking to stray from the path trodden often to find their way to the nooks and crannies of the world.

Five Unseen Destinations

The top 5 hidden gems of the world are included in the list given below for travellers to uncover and explore at their leisure:

Puglia, Italy: The Puglia or Apulia region of the country of Italy is a favourite among travellers as a quaint beauty with quintessential houses and white fences settled among rolling hills and scenic beaches along the Mediterranean coast with fine examples of Baroque architecture rending visitors speechless.

Salta, Argentina: The Salta city in Argentina is well appreciated among travellers for the magnificent examples of Spanish Colonial architecture and the lofty heights of the Andes with remnants of the Inca civilization housed in well-maintained museums which had once flourished in the region.

Ladakh, India: The Ladakh region of India is known only to travellers seeking travel destinations which challenge them at every turn with its winding passes, steep peaks and bitter cold which keep the faint of heart away and allow the visitors to enjoy the snowy plains and frozen glaciers of the cold desert.

Prince Edward Island, Canada: Prince Edward Island is a province of Canada that boasts of beautiful red sand beaches and tall lighthouses dotting them forming a beautiful combination of a beach destination and an opportunity to explore the history and culture that has shaped the country.

Sibiu, Romania: The Sibiu city of Romania is known for its beautiful architecture in Germanic style including medieval walls and a Council Hall as well as other historical artefacts such as paintings and architecture found in museums and cathedrals among trails leading through mountains and ski resorts.

FAQs

What is the nearest airport to Salta in Argentina?

The nearest airport to Salta in Argentina is the Martin Miguel de Güemes International Airport, once known to travellers as the El Aybal Airport, which is located at a distance of seven kilometres or four point three miles to the southwestern direction of the centre of the city and serves over one million passengers travelling to and from the city every year.

What is the nearest airport to Sibiu in Romania?

The nearest airport to Sibiu in Romania is the Sibiu International Airport located in the southern part of the state of Transylvania which is located at a distance of three kilometres or one point nine miles to the West of the city and provides service to over six hundred and seventy-three thousand passengers travelling to and from the city every year.

What is the most important airport in Apulia in Italy?

The most important airport of Apulia is the Bari Karol Wojtyla Airport, also known to travellers as the Palese Airport, which is located in the Bari city of the region at a distance of eight kilometres or five miles in the northwestern direction from the town centre and serves over six million passengers travelling to and from the region every year. Another popular airport of the region is the Brindisi Airport, also known to travellers as the Brindisi Papola Casale Airport as well as the Salento Airport which is located in the Brindisi city at a distance of six kilometres or three point seven miles from the city centre and serves over three million passengers travelling to and from the region every year.

What is the most important airport of Prince Edward Island in Canada?

The most important airport of Prince Edward Island province of Canada is the Charlottetown Airport of the Charlottetown city of the region located at a distance of three point five miles or five point six kilometres in the northern direction from the city and serves over three hundred and sixteen thousand passengers travelling to and from the province every year.

What is the nearest airport in Ladakh in India?

The nearest airport in Ladakh in India is the Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport, named in honour of the nineteenth Kushok Bakula Rimpochee, an important spiritual and diplomatic figure of the area, which provides services to over one million passengers travelling to and from the capital city Leh of Ladakh from its location in the same city.
