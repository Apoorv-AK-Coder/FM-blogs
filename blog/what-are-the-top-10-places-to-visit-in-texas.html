Top 10 Amazing Destinations of Texas

Texas is a very popular tourist spot due to the many attractions dotted in and around the state. This prompts travellers from all over the world to flock to the state to appreciate both its cultural and natural wonders as well as its unique culture. Here is a list of places that you must visit when you catch a Last Minute Flights to Texas.

Austin – Austin is the capital and an important city of the state of Texas with multiple tourist attractions such as the ones listed below:

  • The Contemporary Austin – Laguna Gloria – The Contemporary Austin – Laguna Gloria is a beautiful building made in the Italianate style that was once the home of Clara Driscoll and is located on the banks of the Austin Lake
  • The Paramount Theatre – The Paramount Theatre is a famous building of the city built in the early twentieth century in the classical revival style of architecture that operates both as a cinema hall and a live theatre venue.

Dallas – Dallas is one of the best-known and most populated cities of the state with multiple tourist attractions such as the ones listed below:

  • The Dallas County Courthouse – The Dallas County Courthouse, locally known as the Old Red Museum and the Old Red Courthouse, is a popular tourist attraction in Dallas because of its famous red sandstone structure built in the late nineteenth century.
  • The Perot Museum of Nature and Science – The Perot Museum of Nature and Science is located in Dallas city and attracts many tourists interested in science and the preservation of the environment.

Fort Worth – Fort Worth is another well-known and highly populated city of the state with multiple tourist attractions such as the ones listed below:

  • St. Patrick Cathedral – The St. Patrick Cathedral is a beautiful structure built in the late nineteenth century and the building, its rectory, and the St. Ignatius Academy are considered one of the greatest architectural wonders of the city.
  • The Fort Worth Water Gardens – The Fort Worth Water Gardens are a popular recreation and leisure spot for both locals and tourists of the city with the urban park being termed as an oasis with three pools of water and beautiful terrace gardens.

Houston – Houston is the most highly populated city in the Texas state. It has multiple beautiful tourist attractions such as the ones listed below:

  • Sam Houston Monument – The Sam Houston Monument is a beautiful bronze sculpture made by Enricco Cerrachio stands in the Hermann Park of the city and is considered an important defining feature of the metropolitan area.
  • The Museum of Fine Arts – The Museum of Fine Arts in the city of Houston is an important structure that seeks to preserve and promote the rich history of fine arts and is considered one of the largest art museums in the world.

The state also has some beautiful natural assets such as the ones mentioned here:

  • B. A. Steinhagen Reservoir – The B. A. Steinhagen Reservoir is a beautiful spot for rest located in the Piney Woods near the Jasper Town of the state and conserves the water of the Neches and Angelina Rivers.
  • The Palo Duro Canyon – Palo Duro Canyon, also known as the Grand Canyon of Texas is a well-known geographic feature of the land which has steep sides with multilayered rocks and a deep gorge which makes it a popular tourist spot.

FAQs

What is the pre-colonial history of Texas?

Texas had been the home of many influential and important indigenous cultures for many years including the Ancestral Puebloans, the Mississippian and the MesoAmerican before the arrival of the colonial powers of Europe.

What are the states that border the state of Texas?

Texas is a state that has both national and international borders. The state shares national borders with Louisiana in the east, Arkansas in the northeast, Oklahoma in the north and New Mexico in the west. The state shares international borders with the states of Chihuahua, Coahuila, Tamaulipas and Nuevo León of Mexico.
