Solo Traveling - A Thorough Guide

For travellers, solo journeys are not always a matter of choice but may also arise as a need from unfortunate circumstances. In all situations, the tips included in the list below will help travellers get a grip on how to plan a solo trip and arrange a smooth trip on their own to and from any location of their choice:

  1. Travellers need to start their preparations from the moment they plan the trip and remember the factor of solo travel in every decision taken beginning from the location you intend to head to, the routes and means of transportation you intend to take and the accommodation you will need at the location and make sure they are secure.
  2. Travellers should make sure to carry only their essentials to avoid packing more luggage than they will be able to carry since they need to remember that they shall be alone during the journey and will not be able to share the burden of carrying the luggage with any trusted friend or family member.
  3. Travellers should set out an itinerary for each day with a detailed plan of the amount of time spent at each location or for each activity taking care to account for the amount of time needed in travelling to and from every destination and try to follow the plan as closely as possible so that you do not deviate significantly from the information you have provided your trusted circle.
  4. Travellers should check the recharge plan of their phones before setting out on the journey to be able to contact back home as well as the internet connection at the location they are at to be able to access various services online such as maps to navigate the location or make video calls in case of any emergency.
  5. Travellers should carry their identification information as per the demands of the location and always be alert to avoid any risk of misplacing them. This information will become useful in case the traveller faces an accident or some other emergency where they are unable to identify themselves or provide contact information of their close circle.
  6. Travellers should try to make sure that they check the reviews of the location they are headed to in order to prevent any bad experiences. If you need to make any impromptu stop, make sure to run a quick search on the internet before choosing to avail accommodation at any location and choose your destination before the arrival of dusk.

Things you need to carry for your solo trip

Packing is a very important aspect of a trip and travellers heading on a solo trip should make sure to carry everything they might need for the journey and remain prepared for all situations:

  • One important item that is mandatory for every solo traveller is the first aid kit which includes basic medication for common diseases and allergies and other items needed to treat minor injuries cuts and bruises.
  • Another important item especially during solo travel for women is basic defence items such as a mini pepper spray they can easily carry with themselves or a small knife in case of any untoward occurrence.
  • Travellers should also make sure to carry apparel fit for travel such as comfortable clothes, and shoes suited for the area they are headed to such as sandals for beaches or boots for hikes and treks and headgear to prevent the glare of the sun.

Contact information you need for the trip

The people or organizations that travellers might need to contact during their journey should be carefully saved on their phones and if possible also written down in various other locations to ensure that it is always available to travellers whenever they might need it.

Travellers should put their close circle such as trusted friends and family members on speed dial to ensure that they are always accessible. Travellers should also keep the phone numbers of local law enforcement authorities and other relevant agencies that cater to the needs of travellers on speed dial. Travellers should also save the phone numbers of the hotels or rentals they intend to seek accommodation at to have local contacts in case of any emergency.

Means of transportation for solo travel

Travellers making solo trips should try to remain aware of the amount of time taken by their means of transportation so that they do not exceed the solo travel hours. For this reason, it is best to make reservations for tickets on flights and rented cars early in the day so that even a delay will not force you to travel after dark.

Conclusion

While travelling alone might be a stressful experience due to the sheer amount of work you have to do alone to make it possible, travellers will find enjoyment in the time they get to spend with themselves in the location of their choice. Planning for the trip is the first step to reducing this stress and enjoying the freedom that comes with the trip.
